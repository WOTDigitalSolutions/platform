**Phase:** 0 - Alignment

## Overview

This document defines the primary user personas for WOT Digital Solutions' three product tiers. These personas guide design decisions, feature prioritization, and marketing strategies.

---

## Persona 1: The Emerging Minister

**Product Tier:** Ministry Starter Site ($120-$250)

### Demographics
- **Role:** Fellowship leader, evangelist, new church planter
- **Ministry Size:** 15-50 people
- **Tech Proficiency:** Low to medium
- **Budget:** Limited ($100-$300 for digital tools)
- **Location:** Nigeria, Ghana, Kenya (primarily English-speaking Africa)

### Goals
- Establish online presence quickly
- Share sermons and event information
- Collect prayer requests and partner support
- Build credibility with potential members

### Pain Points
- No technical skills to build a website
- Can't afford expensive web designers
- Generic templates don't reflect ministry vision
- Needs something that "just works"

### User Story
> "I started this fellowship 6 months ago. We meet in a school hall. I want people to find us online, listen to our messages, and support the work. I don't know coding, and I can't spend months waiting for a website."

### Success Metrics
- Site live within 7-10 days
- At least 3 sermons uploaded
- Online giving/partnership functional
- 20+ visitors in first month

## Persona 2: The Ministry Team Leader

**Product Tier:** Ministry Hub ($350-$600)

### Demographics
- **Role:** Senior pastor, ministry coordinator, departmental head
- **Ministry Size:** 50-300 people
- **Team:** 5-20 leaders/workers
- **Tech Proficiency:** Medium
- **Budget:** Moderate ($300-$700)

### Goals
- Centralize ministry operations
- Track team reports (attendance, outreach, cell groups)
- Organize and distribute resources (manuals, training materials)
- Reduce WhatsApp chaos and scattered spreadsheets
- Get actionable insights from ministry data

### Pain Points
- Team sends reports via WhatsApp (hard to track)
- Resources are scattered across Google Drive, Dropbox, email
- No visibility into attendance trends or outreach effectiveness
- Can't easily generate monthly summaries for leadership

### User Story
> "I have 12 cell leaders sending me attendance reports every week via text. I have sermon notes in Drive, training manuals in Dropbox, and prayer requests in email. I need ONE place where everything is organized, and where I can see what's working."

### Success Metrics
- All team members log in within first 2 weeks
- 80% of reports submitted through portal (not WhatsApp)
- Resources organized and accessible
- Admin can export monthly summaries in under 5 minutes

## Persona 3: The Network Apostle

**Product Tier:** Apostolic Network Portal ($700-$1000+)

### Demographics
- **Role:** Apostolic overseer, senior minister, network founder
- **Ministry Reach:** Multiple churches, branches, or campuses (3-50 locations)
- **Team:** 20-200+ leaders
- **Tech Proficiency:** Medium to high (has admin support)
- **Budget:** Established ($700-$2000+)

### Goals
- Centralized oversight of entire network
- Real-time visibility into all branch activities
- Secure resource distribution to pastors and leaders
- Automated reporting and growth analytics
- Professional, scalable system that reflects movement's maturity

### Pain Points
- Managing multiple branches is chaotic
- Each pastor uses different tools (WhatsApp, Excel, email)
- No unified reporting standard
- Hard to identify growth trends or problem areas
- Announcements and resources don't reach everyone consistently
- Current tools don't scale with network growth

- Network-wide dashboard showing key metrics
- Automated monthly summaries reducing admin time by 70%

## Cross-Persona Insights

### Common Needs
- **Simplicity:** All personas need intuitive, "ministry-logic" interfaces
- **Mobile-first:** Most users access via smartphones
- **Reliability:** Must work consistently without technical support
- **Purpose-built:** Generic business tools don't fit ministry workflows

### Key Differentiators
- **Emerging Minister:** Needs credibility + basic functionality
- **Team Leader:** Needs organization + insights
- **Network Apostle:** Needs control + scalability

### Design Principles
1. **Ministry-first language:** Use "sermons" not "content," "branches" not "locations"
2. **Low friction:** Minimize clicks, pre-populate forms, use sensible defaults
3. **Offline-resilient:** Handle poor internet connectivity gracefully
4. **Multi-device:** Seamless experience across phone, tablet, desktop
